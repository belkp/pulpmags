Her Great Moment By Floria Howe Bruess SYLVIA!" a voice called in joy- ous surprise. Esther Dorn, sitting on a bench in the park, looked at the young man who was apparently hailing her. As she stared, his chauffeur brought the car to a stop and the young man sprang out and crossed the road to the girl. His face was eager, his eyes alight. "It's either mistaken identity or an attempt at a pick-up," Esther thought swiftly. "But he looks sin- cere and glad to see--Sylvia." Esther was lonely. It was Sun- day. There was nothing to do. She did not care to go to a concert or show alone. She had gone for a walk in the park, had become tired, and had sat down on a bench.
