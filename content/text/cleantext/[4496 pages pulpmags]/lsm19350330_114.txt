Coming up from the camp below, his foot- steps softened by the matted grass, he was standing almost beside her before she heard him. It was that strange, sweet haunting of his nearness, that brought her swinging breathlessly toward him. For he said noth- ing at all. He simply stared down at her with eyes too sad for anger. Suddenly Bobbie came upon Honey. She was lying face down in the dirt and crying, her shoulders shaking with sobs. "I can't do it! I thought I could blow up the old creek, but I don't know how to set it off!"
