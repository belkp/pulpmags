Fires Of Love By Patricia Ellsworth GLORIA slid her small, trim foot into the second of the scarlet moccasins the old squaw had brought in that morning, and stood up to inspect herself in the mirror. A mischievous smile dimpled about her Cupid's-bow mouth. Her warm brown eyes danced. She was thinking of the tall, red- haired young fellow with whom, for the past two weeks, she had been playing the old, old game of love, but in a new fashion. Gloria knew that "Red" McKenzie was inter- ested--he couldn't help being. Men up here in this North country were no different than they were on Park Avenue.
