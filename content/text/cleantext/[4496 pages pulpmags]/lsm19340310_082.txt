First Payment On Paradise By Winifred Wadell SHE could hear him puttering about in his room as she lifted her hand to knock. Even after she had rapped on the door, she al- most turned back to the safety of her own apartment at the end of the hall. She didn't know his last name. He was an inventor, and his friends called him "Jerry." That was all she knew, except that she had been vaguely aware of his dis- approval the few times they had chanced to meet. Jerry looked like the sort to con- demn her for running around with a bunch of carefree artists, more in- terested in good times than in art. He was frightfully serious about his work.
