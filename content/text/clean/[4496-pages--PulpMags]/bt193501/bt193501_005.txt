PAID IN FULL By GRACE BARLEE It was Saturday night in happy Paris. Marcelle Abadie yawned lazily, and moving across the room to the radio, snapped on the switch and twirled the dial to her favorite station. Her every motion was languid, mechanical. "Alphonse," she murmured, her voice a bare shade higher than the soft tone of the music, "have you ever been told that as a conversationalist you are an exceptional bore?" The man seated across from her leapt to his feet, and regarded her angrily. "Nom d'un nom! Then you consider it boresome to listen to a man's declara- tion of love?"
